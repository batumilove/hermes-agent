# International / Expat Health Insurance Discovery: European Providers

Scope: discovery-only European-provider shortlist relevant to a German citizen family of four living in Georgia. This file captures candidate insurers/plans and public evidence sources. It does not rank providers or build a recommendation dashboard.

Family context used for relevance screening:
- German citizen family of four
- Resident in Georgia
- Adults age 42 and 37
- Two children
- Preferred cover geography: Georgia and Germany, ideally worldwide excluding the US

## European provider section

1. Allianz Care
- Official source: https://www.allianzcare.com/en/personal-international-health-insurance.html
- Quote path: https://my.allianzcare.com/myquote/5
- Evidence: Allianz says its Care plans are designed for people who spend long periods overseas and targets individuals, families, students, nomads, professionals, and expats living, working, or studying abroad.
- Discovery note: strong expat-family candidate; Georgia/Germany fit and worldwide-ex-US configuration still need quote or brochure verification.

2. AXA Global Healthcare
- Official source: https://www.axaglobalhealthcare.com/en/international-health-insurance/
- Quote path: https://www.axaglobalhealthcare.com/en/international-health-insurance/get-a-quote/?camp=online-default
- Evidence: AXA markets the product for people living abroad or travelling overseas frequently and publicly says customers can choose whether to include the USA.
- Discovery note: strong candidate for later worldwide-ex-US validation because the geography toggle is visible on the official page.

3. Bupa Global
- Official source: https://www.bupaglobal.com/en/private-health-insurance
- Quote path: same page / selector-driven
- Evidence: Bupa Global's official individuals-and-families selector publicly lists both Georgia and Germany in the residence-country dropdown.
- Discovery note: one of the strongest Europe-based candidates for this family context because both target countries are visible on the official selector.

4. APRIL International

- Entry path: https://www.april-international.com/en
- Evidence: APRIL positions the product for expatriates and globally mobile people staying abroad longer than 12 months, says policies are annually renewable, and references worldwide cover depending on selected geographic area.
- Discovery note: strong long-term expat candidate; Germany is visible in extracted country examples, Georgia still unverified in this run.

5. MSH International

## Supplemental candidates

These are additional niche, regional, or broker-listed leads captured for later validation. They are not ranked and should be treated as discovery backups until eligibility and policy wording are checked.

1. PassportCard
- Official source: https://www.passportcard.de/en/international-health-insurance/
- Evidence: PassportCard says it offers international private health insurance for expats and nomads, advertises worldwide medical coverage for long-term stays abroad, and publicly shows Expat Basic, Expat Comprehensive, and Executive plan tiers with example EUR prices.
- Discovery note: useful Germany-facing niche lead with unusually visible plan structure, but Georgia residency and exact area-of-cover rules still need verification.

2. VUMI
- Official source: https://www.vumigroup.com/
- Evidence: VUMI markets international health insurance with worldwide coverage, says it protects you and your family anywhere anytime, and lists multiple VIP medical plan families with a Europe region selector.
- Discovery note: relevant global-family backup candidate; geography specifics and European retail availability remain uncertain.

3. Integra Global
- Broker source: https://www.internationalinsurance.com/integra-global/
- Evidence: the broker page describes Integra as international health insurance for expatriates and families and explicitly lists coverage areas of worldwide and worldwide excluding the U.S., Canada, and their territories.
- Discovery note: strong supplemental lead because the broker evidence is unusually specific on family plans and a non-US coverage geography, but official eligibility proof is still needed.

4. MediHelp International
- Official source: https://www.medihelp-assistance.com/en/medihelp-plans/individual-plans
- Evidence: MediHelp's My Global Health page says it offers international coverage and top medical services worldwide, freedom to choose doctor and clinic, and five individual plan tiers.
- Discovery note: interesting Central/Eastern-Europe regional candidate that may be worth later screening for Georgia-resident eligibility.

5. ALC Health
- Official source: https://www.alchealth.com/
- Evidence: ALC Health advertises global health and international medical insurance for expatriates, local nationals, and international travellers worldwide, with an Individuals & Families path and a visible migration to IMG for new business.
- Discovery note: established niche IPMI brand worth retaining in the ledger, though current sales routing and product continuity need follow-up.

6. Blue Cross Blue Shield Global Solutions / former GeoBlue Xplorer
- Broker source: https://www.internationalinsurance.com/geoblue/xplorer/
- Evidence: the broker page says the plans serve expatriates, global citizens, and families residing overseas, and describes an Outside U.S. option that excludes U.S. coverage.
- Discovery note: relevant but more conditional because the product family appears U.S.-linked; direct eligibility for a German family resident in Georgia remains unverified.

## Explicit uncertainties
- This section is discovery-only and intentionally stops before recommendation ranking.
- Many insurers gate exact pricing, underwriting, area-of-cover options, and eligibility behind quote forms or PDF policy documents.
- For most providers, Georgian residency acceptance and a true worldwide-excluding-US configuration were not directly verifiable from the public landing pages reviewed in this run.
- Bupa Global has the clearest public country evidence in this run because both Georgia and Germany appear on the official selector.
